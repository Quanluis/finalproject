Reasons for kappa

Keeping children away.

Many people believe that the Kappa was used to scare children from entering the japenese ponds. Parents would teach their children that the kappa would drown them if they got too close to the ponds and rivers. In reality japenese parents would abondon their undesirable children in these waters, giving historians a reason to believe they didnt want their children discovering the aftermath.

Portuguese monks

The monks came to japan in 1549, their bald heads and pale skin caused many to see a similar resemblance to this created monster, seeing a sort of cultural resistnce to outside foreiners.

japenese Giant Salamander

Some historians claim that the Kappa originated from the fear of Japenese Giant Salamander.They're known to grab their prey with their powerful jaws and submerge them into the waters. They're also known to inhabit the same areas of the Kappa such as rivers and ponds.
